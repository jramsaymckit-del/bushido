BUSHIDO

the way of daily discipline
道

This app was built on a simple conviction: that your daily habits, practised consistently, are the truest expression of who you are becoming. BUSHIDO draws on the Japanese philosophy of the warrior's way — not conquest, but mastery of self through steady, intentional practice. It connects your largest vision to your smallest daily action through a cascade of purpose.

The Four Pillars

流儀
Ryughi — Your Way
The strands of your identity
2–5 threads that define who you are and who you are becoming. Not goals — qualities. "Health & Vitality." "Creative Expression." "Family Bonds." These are the currents running beneath everything you do.
生き甲斐
Ikigai — Your Purpose
Vision, milestones, and this year's goals
Begin with a vivid 10-year vision. Then: what must be in motion at year five? What must this year accomplish? Ikigai gives your daily practice its reason for existing.
職人
Shokunin — Your Craft
The skills you are sharpening
The artisan does not rush toward mastery. Shokunin tracks your monthly and weekly crafts — running, writing, learning, building — each linked to an annual goal. The Shu-Ha-Ri scale marks your progress: Learn → Break → Transcend.
改善
Kaizen — Daily Practice
The small habits that compound
塵も積もれば山となる — even dust accumulates into mountains. Kaizen are your non-negotiable daily and weekly practices. Small enough to do even on bad days. Powerful enough to change everything over time.

Getting Started

1
Complete the onboarding meditation
When you first open BUSHIDO, you'll be guided through a meditation that builds your personal cascade — from Ryughi strands through to daily habits. Take your time. There are no wrong answers, only honest ones.
2
Open the app every day
The Kaizen tab is your home. Check off habits as you complete them. Write a line in the daily reflection. Come back tomorrow. That's the whole practice.
3
Reflect weekly and monthly
A prompt appears on Sunday evenings and during the last three days of each month. These reflections are where the real growth happens — reading your week back to yourself before writing about it.

The things that make daily practice become genuine growth.

The Reflection Cascade

Your daily notes feed your weekly reflection. Your weekly summaries feed your monthly. Your monthly entries appear at the top of your annual. You arrive at each larger reflection already holding all the smaller ones.

The weekly reflection also closes with one intention for the week ahead — shown at the top of next week's reflection, holding you accountable to your past self.

Other Features Worth Knowing

道
The 道 Seal — Stats Easter Egg
Scroll to the very bottom of the scroll. Click the faint 道 seal. Your practice statistics unfold — current streak, personal best, lifetime sessions, and 30-day completion rate for every habit. Grouped by craft. A quiet archive of how far you've come.
☽
Dark Mode
After 9PM the parchment scroll shifts to warm charcoal automatically. The red accents stay. Everything else inverts. Your evening practice is easier on the eyes. Reverts to daylight at 6AM. No setting needed.
☁
Cloud Sync
Scroll to the bottom and tap ☁ sync. Enter your Master Key once per device and your scroll follows you everywhere — MacBook, iPhone, wherever you open the URL. Your data is stored privately in the cloud.
↓
Export & Import
The ↓ export button saves your entire scroll as a dated JSON backup. Store it somewhere safe. If you ever need to restore — new phone, new browser — ↑ import loads it back instantly. Never lose your practice history.

A note on streaks

If you forget to check off a habit and fill it in the next day using the week grid, your streak is preserved. The app reads your actual completion history, not just what you tapped today. Retroactive fills count.

Celebrations

Complete all of today's habits and the 道 seal at the bottom animates. Complete a full Mon–Sun week and the proverb 塵も積もれば山となる rises across the screen. Complete a month at 90%+ and the scroll glows red with your exact percentage. These are earned, not given.
